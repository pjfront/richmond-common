You are given the full auto-generated transcript from a YouTube recording of a Richmond, California City Council meeting. The transcript has timestamps every ~30 seconds.

Write 5-8 paragraphs. Each paragraph covers one major discussion, decision, or public comment period. Use plain language at a 6th-grade reading level.

1. Lead with the single most consequential or contested item — the thing that took the most time, drew the most speakers, or produced the most debate.
2. Each paragraph is 2-4 sentences, max 40 words per sentence.
3. Use PAST tense: "The council voted..." "Residents urged..."
4. When council members debated, capture the substance: "**Martinez** argued the timeline was too aggressive. **Willis** countered that delays would increase costs."
5. For public comment periods, convey what residents actually said — their concerns, arguments, and tone. "Multiple speakers raised concerns about **traffic safety** near the school, with one parent describing a near-miss incident."

7. For vote outcomes, be precise: "Approved 5-2" or "Passed unanimously." Name dissenters.
8. For financial items, always include the dollar amount and the entity.
9. If the meeting had a notable moment (heated exchange, emotional testimony, standing ovation, walkout), include it — these moments matter for understanding what happened.
10. Distinguish open forum (general public comment) from item-specific comment. Open forum topics that drew multiple speakers deserve their own paragraph.
11. Never editorialize. Never say "controversial," "important," or "significant." Let the facts and quotes create weight.
12. Use "the council" not "City Council" or "the Richmond City Council."
13. Use **bold** (markdown) on key terms: council member names, dollar amounts, entity names, topic keywords. A reader who only reads the bold text should grasp the meeting's arc.
14. If the transcript quality is poor (garbled sections, missing audio), note which portions were unclear rather than guessing.
15. Consent calendar items mentioned briefly in transcript get ONE sentence: "The council approved **N routine items** on consent."
16. If a meeting ran unusually long or short, note the duration context.
